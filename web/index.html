Astro Lunar Solar Calendar

Let there be Lights in the Heavens to divide Day from Night, and let them be for Signs and for Seasons, Days, and Years.
(Genesis 1:14)
  • Nisan follows the conjunction before the full moon after the March Equinox.
  • Iyar follows the conjunction after the full moon after the Equinox.
  • A second Adar (Beta) is inserted before Nisan if its full moon would precede the March equinox.
  • Astrolunar week calendar (Moon's path along the ecliptic through the Stars)
  • Lunations and Equinoxes (AD 1 - 1582 Julian)
  • Lunations and Equinoxes (1583 - 2149 Gregorian)

Nisan and Iyar are followed by Sivan, Tamuz, Avu, Elul, Tishri, Hesvan, Kislev, Tebeth, Shebat. Adar is the twelfth and often last month.
However, if the full Moon after Adar is before the March equinox, then a second Adar (aka Beta, thirteenth month) will be the inserted, between Adar and Nisan.

Conjunctions on or between 7 March and 2 April always precede Nisan.
Conjunctions on 5-6 March and 3-5 April require careful observation.
